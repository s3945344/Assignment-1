INTEREST IN I.T

What is your interest in IT? When did your interest in IT start?

Ever since a young age, I have always been interested in technology, and enjoyed being able to learn and pick up new computer skills, so I was encouraged by my parents to consider IT as a potential pathway option. My first proper encounter with IT was in Year 10, when I chose to study IT as one of my electives. Prior to this, I had no idea of what IT actually was and what it offered, as well as what possible pathways it could lead to if I chose to pursue it. However, throughout my time studying this elective, In particular, I had always enjoyed learning the design aspect of IT, such as web development and the programming aspect as well. I always thought the idea of working on something and seeing the final result was very satisfying and I liked that feeling of accomplishment, hence why those aspects of IT always stood out to my liking.



Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest? Outline your IT experience.

There was a particular person who sparked my interest, this person was my IT teacher throughout Year 10 to Year 12. She played a significant role in my interest in IT and she was also someone who believed in me when I had a moment of doubt about whether IT was the right career pathway for me. Through her, I learned how to use a wide range of software programs and because she made learning about IT fun, it allowed my passion for the subject to continue to grow. With her, I studied IT in Year 10 and then chose to study Applied Computing: Data Analytics in VCE. In VCE, I learned more about how to work with databases and was introduced briefly to programming, which I grew to really enjoy.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

I was first introduced to RMIT through my brother, who studied Landscape Architecture. Thanks to him, I was able to have a brief overview of the type of university experience RMIT had to offer and I became set on wanting to study at RMIT throughout the entirety of high school - this was regardless of what course I planned on studying as I found the RMIT community very welcoming and exciting. Another reason I chose to come to RMIT was because of the travel time from where I live. RMIT, compared to all the other universities in Melbourne, is the most convenient as my campus is in the city, which means I can take a thirty-to-forty minute train ride without stress.
